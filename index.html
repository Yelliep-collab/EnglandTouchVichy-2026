Welcome to Vichy 2026

The European Touch Championships return for the largest Euros in history.

Hosted by Touch France on behalf of FIT, the event runs from Tuesday 21 to Saturday 25 July 2026 across thirteen recognised playing divisions. England Touch travels in numbers, fielding fourteen touring teams across the Opens, Seniors and Masters categories — one of the broadest squads England has ever assembled for a European campaign.

The Venue

Parc Omnisports Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes — Vichy

All games are played at the Parc Omnisports in Bellerive-sur-Allier. Set in around 120 hectares of wooded parkland on the banks of Lake Allier — one of the largest multi-sports complexes in Europe, used in the build-up to the Paris 2024 Olympic Games.

The Town of Vichy

Queen of the Spa Towns

Queen of the Water Towns

Vichy sits on the River Allier in the Auvergne. Emperor Napoleon III transformed Vichy in the 1860s into the elegant resort it remains today — earning it the nickname "Reine des Villes d'Eaux". A compact, walkable town of Belle Époque grandeur, leafy riverside parks and a relaxed spa-town pace.

What to See and Do

  • Hall des Sources — the Belle Époque pavilion where thermal springs are piped for tasting.
  • Les Parcs de Vichy & the Allier esplanade — riverside promenades, cafés, cycling, and a town beach in summer.
  • Palais des Congrès-Opéra — Vichy's ornate opera house (lit up in summer for the "Lights of Bourbonnais" show).
  • Boulevard des États-Unis & the Imperial Chalets — a parade of flamboyant 19th-century villas.
  • Église Saint-Blaise — Art Deco and Byzantine styles, with a Black Madonna.
  • Source des Célestins — the most famous of the springs; tour the pavilion.

Quick Facts

RegionAllier, Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, central France
Known ForThermal springs, Belle Époque architecture, Vichy Célestins water
Nearest CityClermont-Ferrand (with onward rail links to Paris)
Getting AroundCompact and walkable; electric buses, tourist train and river boat

Accommodation

Two historic hotels in the heart of Vichy

A Note on Our Hotels

England's accommodation is a step above what you might expect — and we want to be transparent about why. England Touch followed the official FIT/Touch France hotel allocation process, however there was a miscommunication and other nations booked independently, which significantly reduced available options. The remaining accommodation consisted of approximately 50 separate Airbnbs scattered across town, or a single block 45 minutes outside Vichy. Neither was acceptable.

Touch France worked hard to find a solution at a comparable cost. The hotel may look like an upgrade, but it has not come at any additional expense to athletes. All 14 touring teams are kept across just two hotels, 5 minutes walk apart.

Hotel 1: Aletti Palace Hôtel

The last surviving Belle Époque palace hotel still operating in Vichy, beside the Opera House and Parc des Sources.

  • Historic palace atmosphere
  • Outdoor swimming pool and traditional French restaurant
  • Prime location opposite the Opera House
  • The hotel does not have a parking lot.

Hotel 2: Célestins Spa Thermal & Hôtel

Vichy's flagship modern luxury wellness hotel, directly connected to one of Europe's largest thermal spa complexes.

  • 5-star wellness hotel
  • Direct access to thermal baths and spa treatments
  • Located beside Napoléon III Park and the Allier waterfront
  • Public underground parking is available at the hotel at €16.00 per 24 hours per vehicle.

Insurance

England Touch Membership Insurance — What You're Covered For

What Your Membership Insurance Covers

  • Personal Accident Cover — For members under 80 injured whilst taking part in approved Touch Rugby activities.
  • Public Liability Insurance — Protection against claims from third parties during sanctioned England Touch activities.
  • Professional Indemnity Cover — For coaching, refereeing or other approved roles.
  • Club and Volunteer Protection — Employers' liability, products liability, directors' & officers' liability.
Full details: portal.sports-insure.co.uk/england-touch

What Is Not Covered

  • Medical Treatment & Rehabilitation — Not designed to provide comprehensive private medical treatment or physiotherapy costs.
  • Loss of Earnings — Lost income if an injury prevents you from working.
  • Personal Property & Equipment — Damage to or theft of personal belongings, luggage, or sporting equipment.
  • Travel-Related Risks — Standard membership insurance is not a comprehensive travel protection.

GHIC / EHIC — Travelling to Europe

Athletes travelling to Europe should obtain a valid Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC) to access medically necessary state-provided healthcare in participating European countries.

Apply at: NHS.uk — Apply for a free GHIC

Passports & Travel

Essential reading — check your passport well before departure

⚠️ Passport Validity — British Passport Holders

France follows Schengen area rules. Your passport must have a date of issue less than 10 years before the date you arrive, and an expiry date at least 3 months after the date you plan to leave the Schengen area.

Quick check: "Date of Issue" — if it says anything before July 2016, it will not be accepted. "Expiry Date" — must show a date after 26 October 2026.

EU Entry/Exit System (EES)

EES has been fully operational since 10 April 2026 — in full effect when we travel to Vichy. EES replaces manual passport stamping; you may need to register biometric details (fingerprints and a photo) on your first Schengen entry. No action needed before the border. Your digital EES record is valid for 3 years.

At the Border — What to Have Ready

  • Your valid passport
  • Proof of travel insurance covering emergency medical costs
  • Tournament/tour information (hotel booking confirmation or team manager's contact details)
  • Evidence of a return journey

Non-British Passport Holders

Some nationalities require a Schengen visa regardless of UK residency status. Non-British citizens should check the French Embassy website or contact the England Touch logistics team as early as possible.

Contact: logistics@englandtouch.org.uk
